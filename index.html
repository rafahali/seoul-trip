Drink

Ranked. Orange-tent soju with off-duty office workers, a bar you reach by climbing five dark floors, another you order from on a 1970s payphone.

1Jongno 3-ga Tent Alley

~$10–15
★ Top three
pojangmachaJongno · 25 min, Line 3walk-in, cash

At dusk the orange-tarp tents bloom along the pavement, plastic stools spilling into the lane, loosened-tie office workers hunched over bubbling odeng broth and sweating soju bottles, the whole strip glowing amber. Order odeng and a green bottle, sit shoulder-to-shoulder with locals, watch the after-work churn. Densest toward Exits 5–6.

2Euljiro Nogari Alley · Manseon Hof

~$10–15
★ Top three

Wander

Ranked. Where to get pulled into something real: old men who'll wave you over for makgeolli, an "old-man Harajuku" flea market, a maker-maze of welders and hidden bars.

1Jongno / Tapgol / Donui-dong makgeolli alley

free–$6
★ Top three
pulled into itJongno · 18 min, Line 3walk-in

The single best place in Seoul to be absorbed into something real, because it was built for old Seoul, not for you: start at Tapgol Park past elderly men in worn fedoras, 5,000-won sidewalk barbers and 1,000-won coffee carts, then thread into the Donui-dong makgeolli alleys, low doorways, charcoal-mackerel smoke, cloudy rice wine in dented kettles, and old men who will absolutely wave you over. That alley is your highest-odds "pulled in" moment of the trip.

Local Tips

The handful of things that make Seoul easy. Read once, then move like you live here.

Maps: use Naver Map (or KakaoMap). Google driving navigation simply doesn't work in Korea. Search in English, it mostly resolves.

Taxis: grab the KakaoT app, the local Uber, cheap and everywhere. Most of this menu is a $4–6 ride from the hotel.

Subway: Line 3 runs straight from Apgujeong to nearly every old-Seoul spot here, no transfer. Buy a T-money card at any convenience store and tap on and off.

Cash: needed for the tents, flea markets, and the nogari alley. Cards work everywhere else.

Don't tip. Anywhere. Korea doesn't do it and it reads as odd. The price is the price.

Translation: download Papago, it beats Google Translate for Korean and has a camera mode that reads menus and signs live.

Reservations: the top restaurants and bars run on the CatchTable app (English, foreign cards). The Reserve buttons land on each spot's date screen. For a 5-top this close in, turn on the in-app waitlist, drops hit 1–2 days out. Mingles needs a deposit and Evett needs an email for five.

Jjimjilbang rules: shower fully nude before the baths, no phones in the bath area, then put on the matching pajamas for the co-ed floors. Yes, do the body scrub.

Clubs: carry passports, and pre-book a table so the door is a non-issue for a group of five.

Convenience stores (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven) are 24h and everywhere: the riverside cup-ramyeon move, cheap hangover drinks, T-money top-ups, umbrellas when the rain hits.
